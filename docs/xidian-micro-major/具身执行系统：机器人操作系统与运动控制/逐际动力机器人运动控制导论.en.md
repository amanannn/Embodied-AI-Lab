## II. FluxVLA Engine — Full-Stack End-to-End VLA Engineering Platform

A full-stack end-to-end engineering platform for embodied AI applications, bridging the complete engineering loop from data to real-robot deployment. Provides a standardized foundation for academia and industry, significantly lowering the engineering barrier for VLA R&D.

### 2.1 Core Design Principles

Unified configuration, standardized interfaces, module decoupling, deployability.

### 2.2 Unified Modular VLA Backbone

All Models inherit from `BaseVLA` — Vision Encoder, Language Encoder, Projection Layer into LLM embedding space, and ActionPrediction Head — enabling seamless switching between the following without rewriting the entire training pipeline:

OpenVLA, LlavaVLA, GR00T, Pi0, Pi0.5

### 2.3 Backbone Network Coverage

| Category | Supported Models |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Language Model** | LLaMA, Gemma, Qwen series |
| **Vision** | DinoSigLIP (DINO + SigLIP) |
| **Vision-Language** | PaliGemma, QwenVL |

### 2.4 Data and Training

| Dimension | Capability |
|-----------|------------|
| **Data Format** | Native support for Parquet and RLDS Pipeline; Multi-dataset Mixed Training for heterogeneous data |
| **Distributed Training** | FSDP and DDP for large-scale tasks |
| **Practical Training Stack** | LoRA, Mixed Precision (AMP), Checkpoint Resume, automatic Evaluation after training |

### 2.5 From Benchmark to Real Robot

| Stage | Capability |
|-------|------------|
| **Evaluation** | Multi-GPU Evaluation, LIBERO (including environments without ray tracing, e.g., A100) |
| **Inference** | Real-robot Inference Script, Inference Mode that skips loading full pretrained weights to save VRAM |

### 2.6 Engineering Architecture

| Module | Content |
|--------|---------|
| **`fluxvla/`** | Model (VLA, Backbone, Head, Projector), Dataset, Transform, Tokenizer, Engine, Optimizer, Collator |
| **`configs/`** | Organized by Model Family (openvla, llava, gr00t, pi0, pi05) |
| **`scripts/`** | End-to-end Training, Evaluation, and Real-robot Inference pipelines |

End-to-end training flow: Parquet/RLDS Loading → Transform + Batch Assembly → Forward → Action Loss → Backward.

### 2.7 Toolchain

| Tool | Version / Notes |
|------|-----------------|
| PyTorch | 2.6 |
| Hugging Face Transformers | 4.53.x |
| Flash Attention | 2.5.x |
| TensorFlow | For RLDS |
| LIBERO | Manipulation, multi-task learning, transfer learning, and VLA research iteration |

### 2.8 Roadmap

More vision/VLM backbones and VLA methods, VLM or Chain-of-Thought (CoT) data training, Isaac Sim integration, and improved logging capabilities.

## IV. Embodied AI Industry: Current State

### 4.1 Deployment Reality

> Very few directions can truly be commercialized. Clear demand exists for: load-bearing and dance. Other areas urgently need exploration.

| Current State | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Hardware Limitations** | Cannot operate continuously for long; joints overheat |
| **Scene Adaptation** | Not designed for specific application scenarios |
| **Potential Markets** | Agriculture, monotonous/boring/dangerous scenarios, food delivery, ride-hailing — viable but needs time |
| **Academic Market** | Selling to university research labs — extremely high tolerance |

### 4.2 Industry Reflection

> Makeshift operations; expectations are too high.

- Industry development trends are not necessarily monotonically increasing — robotics development may resemble deep learning, with ups and downs
- In the up phase, one can benefit from industry dividends — but what to do during the down phase?

### 4.3 Talent Dilemma

| Issue | Current State |
|-------|---------------|
| **What the Industry Needs Most** | Qualified, professional practitioners |
| **The Ordinary Student's Dilemma** | How do students from less-resourced universities, or those who aren't the "elite," truly enter the industry? |
| **Tech Stack** | Fragmented; steep learning curve |
| **Undergraduate Barrier** | High entry threshold |

> Conclusion: one must compete for resources, push hard, and fight for genuinely high-cost resources. Be interest-driven, and engage deeply with open-source projects.
